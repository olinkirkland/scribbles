Charterhall's main thoroughfare is quiet by the time you meet with Basil ruthless, sweaty, owner of the aptly named and aptly spacious ‘Endless Apothecary’, where he proudly declares if a herb or remedy can't be found here, it doesn't exist. Basil leads you through his lengthy store before handing you a leaflet from a binder.

The leaflet reads: ‘Big Announcement! Golden Button Mushrooms are now available at the Golden Harvest Grotto in Barrowcleft. Heal any wound for a small contribution. We do require that our miracle mushroom be consumed on-premises. Signed, Ravena Glenmont’

This came to my attention only last week, and I wouldn't have believed it if I hadn't gone to Barrowcleft and seen it for myself! Get me a sample of the stuff, and make sure they can’t make any more. I'll charge whatever I want when I’m the only source!
Approach & Obstacles

Approach: Golden Harvest has a reputation as Duskwall’s best supplier of high quality edible fungus. The workers call themselves cultivators and are very loyal, treating Golden Harvest less like a place of work and more like a family, or a cult.

  • Homestead: Golden Harvest’s homestead holds the grotto entrance. Wide tables out front showcase “farm-to-table” wares.

    • Padlocked grotto gate only opens from the outside
    • A rusting Hull checks all cultivators going in or out

  • Grotto: A rickety bridge spans a clear lake. Colorful mushrooms sprout from floating gardens.

    • Cultivators in togas work quietly, minimizing sound
    • An unattended diving suit
    • Fishing nets lift writhing masses onto the bridge

  • Hot Springs Cavern: Warm water bubbles up from the cavern’s deepest point. Golden Buttons grow in small clusters.

    • Sarha doesn’t sleep
    • The cavern is a Spirit Well
    • Harvesting Golden Buttons releases spores into the air

Areas: mushroom lounge — director’s office — worker’s lodging — holding cells — Sarha’s shrine
Big Tuna’s underwater cave — composting pit — abandoned mine drift — cave stairs — store room

Scenes & Complications

  • If you like, you may purchase product and consume it in the Hot Springs. Otherwise, I’m afraid I’ll have to ask you to leave.

    • Ravena orders the Hull to throw you out
    • A worker demands your crew disarm
    • You’re prompted to recite a pass-phrase

  • With bare feet, workers carefully navigate the bridge. It creaks under the weight, almost like it’s warning you that crossing will be tricky.

    • You lose your balance and fall into the water
    • A worker approaches you, angrily, mistaking you for someone else. “Clumsy as always!”

  • Sarha grabs your collar and throws you down into the hot spring’s water. You’ve come for the Golden Buttons! You aren’t worthy!

    • The spring water gets much, much hotter
    • You sense an entity from the Spirit Well probing your mental defenses, trying to get in
    • You step into a mushroom cluster, and the air is immediately thick with fungal spores

  • You hear a voice reverberate in your head. Don’t destroy... It’s my only way back in…

    • Spores coalesce into spindly figures that drag you towards the Spirit Well
    • Cultivators start blockading the entrance to the cavern. Sarha said this would happen!
    • The water in the cavern begins to rise
    • You feel sluggish, like you can barely move
Breakfast of Champignons: When consumed, a Golden Button Mushroom always repairs your body and mind, but you’ll also receive one or more unexpected side effects.
A Dose of Golden Button Mushrooms

Optionally, roll (or have players roll) on the table below to determine one or more side effects, then have them choose either to fill their Healing Clock, or to remove a Trauma.

    Side Effects

    • you feel a deep tranquility; relieve 1 Stress
    • you can talk to mushrooms four times *
    • for the next week your skin glows green-blue
    • your worst harm transfers to a random ally
    • +1 to your next Insight action or resistance
    • the Mushroom King is drawn to you **

    Primary Effect

    • Fill your Healing Clock
    • or
    • Remove a Trauma

* Make a supply clock with four segments. For more information on supply clocks, check p. 86 of Deep Cuts
** Start or progress a clock for the Mushroom King's arrival

Cast of Characters

  • Ravena Glenmont is the long-time elected director of Golden Harvest. She doesn’t eat the mushrooms, but is respected for her mutually beneficial, detailed contracts. patient, cunning

    • Wants to lead a successful business and make fruitful deals with other farmers
    • Doesn’t want Golden Harvest to suffer financially or from bad publicity

  • Sarha is the self-proclaimed spiritual leader of Golden Harvest’s cultivators. She has some permanent effects from regular Golden Button use, which once healed bites she received from cave-fishing. somber, spiritual

    • Wants to bring new members into the cult and to share the cult’s meditation rituals
    • Doesn’t want the Hot Springs to be disrespected

  • Big Tuna is a huge, ancient cave fish living in the grotto’s lake. He’s become aggressive with age, and although his eye-sight isn’t what it used to be, there have been some close calls among the cultivators. hungry, armored, slippery

    • Wants to drag live prey into his underwater cave
    • Doesn’t want to hear loud noises or for his territory (the lake) to be disturbed
Clocks & Consequences
  • 6 Cave Fish destroy the Bridge

    Loud noises and other disturbances create turmoil in the lake, driving the cave fish into an angry frenzy.

  • 4 The Cult is Alerted

    The cultivators are numerous and they’re not going to let you leave the grotto without a fight.
